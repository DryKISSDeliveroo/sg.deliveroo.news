As the year comes to a close, food delivery service Deliveroo takes a moment to reflect on the food highlights of 2019 to present the most popular dishes around the world, as chosen by its customers. From bibimbap to burritos - this year's take-away favourites are an eclectic mix of longtime local favourites and trendy innovations.

Here's a taster of the year's top ten dishes on Deliveroo around the world:

  1. Poké Signature Super Protéiné from Pokawa, Paris

  4. Salmon Pokè from Pokèria by NIMA, Milan
  5. Footlong Sub from Subway, Dublin

A Snapshot of Singapore's Most Loved Dishes -

Small in size but big on flavour, Singapore is a foodie haven with lots of options. It's no small feat emerging the most ordered dish in Singapore but The Daily Cut edged out stiff and plentiful competition to clinch the top spot and the award for the "11th Most Ordered Dish" globally for its Build Your Own Bowl. With a wide range of nutritious and delicious ingredients to choose from, the mix-and-match format makes it perfect for a quick and wholesome workday lunch.

The trend of healthy and customisable meals in Singapore continues to gain momentum with VIOS by Blu Kouzina's VIOS Regular Bowl, Maki-San's Little San Sushi and Urban Mix' Build Your Own Mix all securing spots in Singapore's top 15 most ordered dishes.

That's not to say Singaporeans don't indulge their sweet tooth now and then. Gong Cha's Black Milk Tea came out tops as Singapore's most ordered drink, while Yocha Tea & Desserts' Milk Tea and Each A Cup's Grape Red Tea also making an appearance on the list at the 20th and 21st spots respectively.

Singaporeans continue to have an appetite for local dishes such as Beef Hor Fan, Sweet and Sour Pork, Chicken Rice and Hot Plate Beancurd, which came in as the fifth, sixth, seventh and 10th most ordered dishes respectively across the island.

Food Trends around the World -

While pizza was 2018's most ordered food globally, 2019 has seen a shift towards healthier options with the Hawaiian poké bowl emerging first. A closer look at Deliveroo's top 100 reveals some insightful foodie ordering trends over the course of the year.

Health-conscious

With orders of healthy options increasing by 51% this year it's no surprise dishes like the Kale Salad from Hong Kong's KALE or the Chicken Milanese from Simple Health Kitchen in London have made it into the top 100.

Crazy for chicken

Whether fried, grilled or rolled into a burrito, it's clear that people love chicken with poultry-based dishes filling a fifth of all the spots on this year's Deliveroo 100. Favourites include Chicken Rice from Boon Tong Kee, Singapore, Grilled Boneless Chicken from Dubai's Al Mandaloun and the 9 Hotwings Boxmeal from Eindhoven's KFC.

Asian cuisine

This year, a quarter of those featured in the Deliveroo 100 were traditional Asian dishes. Favourites include Bibimbap Viande from Pierre Sang Express in Paris and Sushi roll di salmone e avocado from Basara, Milan.

Tasty traditions

This year's top 100 also showed that people are celebrating their local favourites. Favourite national dishes include Cod and Chips from Harbourside Fish & Chips in Plymouth, Regina Pizza from Milan's Assaje, and Beef Hor Fan from Rasa Rasa, Singapore.
