Deliveroo’s Top 100 Dishes Ordered In 2018 Around the World

  • 13 December 2018
  • Deliveroo
  • Read time: 4 min

As the year draws to a close, food delivery service Deliveroo takes a moment to reflect on the food highlights of 2018, and reveals the most popular dishes around the world, as chosen by its customers.  From bubble tea to burritos – this year's eclectic mix of take-away favourites certainly makes for an interesting read.

Here's a taster of this year's top ten dishes on Deliveroo around the world:

1) Pad Thaï from Thaï at Home, Paris

2) Cheeseburger from Five Guys, London

3) Sushi Lovers Poke Bowl from CALI-POKE, Dubai

4) Burrito from Boojum, Dublin

5) Bubble Tea from Tenren's Tea, Hong Kong

6) Grilled Chicken Burrito from Guzman Y Gomez, Sydney

7) Burrito from Gonzalez & Co, Barcelona

8) Beef Burger from Tommi's Burger Joint, Berlin

9) Poke Bowl from Temakery, Amsterdam

10) 2 Hot Wings® from KFC, Brighton

A Closer Look at Singapore's Most Loved Dishes

Despite having one of the smallest populations out of Deliveroo's markets, our Little Red Dot is a strong contender in the foodie arena, clinching the coveted title of '12th Most Ordered Dish'. The spot was claimed by Maki-San's Mega San Sushi, making it the most ordered dish on Deliveroo in Singapore in 2018.

The fully customizable Mega-San Sushi beat local delight Nasi Ayam Penyet, which came in at number 73. In fact, one customer loved the dish so much they ordered it 30 times over the year (yes, you have read this right). Order figures for 2018 also indicate that Singaporeans are becoming more health conscious, with four of the seven most ordered dishes in Singapore falling under Deliveroo's Healthy Food section.

Deliveroo's data shows that most orders for the healthy stuff came in on Mondays; think salads, noodles and wraps, while orders for burgers, pizzas, fried chicken and sugary treats tend to spike on Fridays and Saturdays. Cheat day anyone? Unsurprisingly, patriotism can be felt most strongly on National Day, with a 12% increase in orders for local dishes such as Nasi Ayam Penyet, Fish Curry and Minced Meat Noodles.

But it's not all about getting our five-a-day, Singaporeans cannot seem to shake that sweet tooth. Not only did bubble tea make it into Singapore's top ten most ordered list, a staggering 450,000 cups of bubble tea were delivered throughout 2018, enough to fill over half of Marina Bay Sands Pool (the world's largest infinity edge pool)!  #TGIF starts early with the highest number of bubble tea orders coming in on Friday between 3 – 5PM.

Food Trends around the World

While globally noodles and Hawaiian poke bowls were trending this year, 2018 also saw healthy plant-based dishes grow in popularity.  A firm favourite last year, pizza remained a top choice for food delivery around the world with five appearing in the top 100. Deliveroo also reports an increase in orders of more traditional takeaway cuisines, with favourites including a Doner Kebab from German Doner Kebab in Birmingham and Satay from Xian Street Food in Galway.

A closer look at Deliveroo's top 100 reveals some fun foodie ordering trends over the course of 2018.

Health Kick

With orders of healthy options almost tripling this year, it's no surprise dishes like The Original vegan Açaí Bowl from the UAE's The Acai Spot or Singapore's Harvest Protein & Grain Bowl from Harvest have made it into the top 100. The most frequently occurring dish in the list, featuring twelve times, from eight different countries is the incredibly instagrammable and equally nutritious Poke Bowl.

Comfort Foods

But it's not all raw fish and salads. Our classic comfort foods - burgers and burritos - fill almost a fifth of the spots on this year's Deliveroo 100, with favourites including Dublin's Boojum Burrito and Germany's Classic Burger from Tommi's Burger Joint.

Sweet Treats

It seems it's not just Singapore which has developed a sweeter tooth this year, with five times more desserts appearing in this years top 100 compared to last, including the Banana Pudding from Dubai-based Magnolia Bakery and Berlin's Happy Waffle from Waffle Brothers.

Local Favourites

This year's top 100 also showed that people are celebrating their local favourites, in addition to the popular high street chains. Favourite national dishes include Tortilla Española from Madrid-based José Luis, Shrimp Dumplings from Dim Dim Sum in Hong Kong and Boulette Sauce Brusseleir from Ballekes in Brussels.
